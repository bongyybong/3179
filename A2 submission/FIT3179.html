Malaysia's Rail Network

We will mainly focus on West Malaysia due to their extensive rail network such as LRT, MRT, and KTM.

The railway network in Malaysia has significantly expanded over the years, with major developments in urban areas like Kuala Lumpur. This expansion includes the construction of various LRT and MRT lines, providing more accessibility to public transportation.

The data presented in the map highlights the population density in different districts, emphasizing the importance of rail access in densely populated regions.

Malaysia is a car-centic country, with very little walkability within a few KM radius of train stations. Busses are also infrequent, leaving the population with no choice but to drive.

Rail ridership in Malaysia has been increasing back to pre-covid levels. The latest MRT3 is said to complete the Klang Valley train loop, as it is a circle loop. This may increase the effectiveness of the system.

Pre-Covid, in 2019, it has achieved 283,290,816 riders. In 2021, it is the lowest with only 78,288,635 riders. In 2023, it has achieved 272,051,582 riders.

This shows that there is a strong demand for public transport.

Demand for the Kelana Jaya Line is high as it is operating at almost 100 percent capacity. Otherwise, other line seems to have decent ridership. By completing the MRT3 project, demand can be spread out into other places, reducing congestion. As such, Malaysia has decent railway connection in urban areas.
